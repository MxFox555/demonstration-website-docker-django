How do I use this?

This tool gives you two series of values which you can then graph. The returned lines being the volatile and stable lines.

These values represent how popular a stock is becoming. A lower value represents more interest in the stock and higher value represents dwinding interest.

The volatile series of data is the most reactive to market conditions and should be used for an initial assessment.

The stable series of data is used to determine how consistently attention is being brought to the stock and moves more conservatively.

FAQ

Does this always predict rises?

This tool makes it's best efforts to predict the interest from other investors a stock is getting. Just because there is interest in a stock it does not mean it will rise.

Is this a standalone tool?

This tool can provide insight by itself but adding it to your own set of metrics and methods is always more beneficial.

How do I access it?

The primary use is through an API. You will recieve your key with your account and further instructions will be provided on the page.

Does this work with blue chip stocks?

Yes. CSL.AX has been provided in the images carousel above as an example. These stocks typically always have interest in them which usually results in slight deviations of the volatile and stable lines.
